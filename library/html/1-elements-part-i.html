Who came up with Socionics?

[Image content]

Aušra Augustinavičiūtė (1927 - 2005)

Pronounced “A-ow-sh-ra A-ow-gus-tee-na-vitch-oot-eer”

Lithuanian economist and dean of the family science department at Vilnius Pedagogical University

Created Socionics to help explain relationship with her husband

Gives her initial to Model ‘A’

All slides are copyrighted by Jack Oliver Aaron of the World Socionics Society

Key Influences

[Image content]

Antoni Ignacy Tadeusz Kępiński (1918 - 1972)

Pronounced “An-tow-nee Kem-pi–ni-ya-skee”

Polish psychologist and philosopher

Founded axiological psychiatry

Originated two key ideas of Socionics:

Information Metabolism

Hierarchy of Values

All slides are copyrighted by Jack Oliver Aaron of the World Socionics Society

Key Influences

[Image content]

Carl Gustav Jung (1875 - 1961)

Pronounced “Ka-arl Goo-stuff Eey-oong”

Swiss psychiatrist and psychoanalyst

Founded analytical psychology and wrote Psychological Types, the basis of Socionics and MBTI

First to discover ‘cognitive functions’

Used cognitive functions to frame personality type

ERRATIC

[Image content]

Perception, Extroverted and Static

Jumps unpredictably all over the place

Comprises many, different objects to acquire

Fickle in focus and hungry for more and more

Also called “flexible-maneuvering”

All slides are copyrighted by Jack Oliver Aaron of the World Socionics Society

TRANQUIL

[Image content]

Perception, Introverted and Dynamic

Gradually shifting and adjusting to balance

Comprises fields that are acted upon

Refines quality of input it passively receives

Also called “receptive-adaptive”

All slides are copyrighted by Jack Oliver Aaron of the World Socionics Society

FIXED

[Image content]

Judgement, Introverted and Static

Remains unbending, until it breaks

Comprises fixed ties and arrangements

Keeps faith with conviction

Also called “balanced-stable”

All slides are copyrighted by Jack Oliver Aaron of the World Socionics Society

RELENTLESS

[Image content]

Judgement, Extroverted and Dynamic

Continuously purposeful and on the move

Comprises ongoing, transformative processes

Always something to do in each situation

Also called “linear-assertive”
