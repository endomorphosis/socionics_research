Type Names (Three Letters)

Letter One

Can be Sensory, Logical, Intuitive or Ethical

Corresponds to the Leading function

Letter Two

Also can be Sensory, Logical, Intuitive or Ethical

Corresponds to the Creative function

Can be either Energiser or Integrator

First Small Group: CLUBS

[Image content]

Grouped by being:

Sensory or Intuitive

Logical or Ethical

Identifying or Transforming

Share the following functions:

Strong & Weak

Succeed or fail in same areas

Tend to have similar careers and hobbies

Intellectuals

[Image content]

Intuitive & Logical Identifiers

Doubly-detached, removed from experiences

Best at abstract thinking

Lose touch with daily social interactions

Natural theorists, strategists, and inventors

ILE, LII, ILI & LIE

Socialites

[Image content]

Sensory & Ethical Identifiers

Doubly-involved, tangled up in experiences

Best at daily social interactions

Get too involved and struggle to think things out

Natural carers, aesthetes, and diplomats

SEI, ESE, SEE & ESI

Realists

[Image content]

Sensory & Logical Transformers

Doubly-external, grounded in reality

Best at real, practical thinking

Lack awareness of what is meaningful or sublime

Natural skilled labourers, technicians and soldiers

LSI, SLE, LSE & SLI

Humanitarians

[Image content]

Intuitive & Ethical Transformers

Doubly-internal, steeped in inner meaning

Best at spiritual reflection, insight into humanity

Struggle to make things work in real life

Natural creatives, psychologists and clerics

Α - Alpha - The Quadra of Infancy

[Image content]

Accepting & Clarifying Identifiers

Attractive elements in Valued functions

Motivation: to discover the universe and unite it in harmony

Likes: fun, curiosity, discovery, beauty, comfort, guiding philosophies

Dislikes: negativity, judgmentalness, arrogance, discord

Intellectuals create and explore theories to explain the universe

Socialites help everyone to get along and have fun

Energisers push enthusiasm and interest in sharing new ideas

Integrators push aesthetic beauty and structural elegance

ILE, SEI, ESE & LII

All slides are copyrighted by Jack Oliver Aaron of the World Socionics Society

Β - Beta - The Quadra of Youth

[Image content]

Rejecting & Clarifying Transformers

Conclusive elements in Valued functions

Motivation: to impactfully and meaningfully change the universe

Likes: passion, power, decisiveness, destiny, certainty, codes of honour

Dislikes: equivocation, hypocrisy, banality, quiet disapproval

Humanitarians express their vision to inspire and uplift society

Realists fight to win and enforce systems of law and order on society

Energisers push a will to power and amplification of their voice

Integrators push an uncompromising worldview and higher purpose

EIE, LSI, SLE & IEI

All slides are copyrighted by Jack Oliver Aaron of the World Socionics Society

Γ - Gamma - The Quadra of Maturity

[Image content]

Rejecting & Integrifying Identifiers

Repulsive elements in Valued functions

Motivation: to resist a dangerous world and achieve independence from it

Likes: loyalty, initiative, scepticism, competence, prudence, discernment

Dislikes: unreliability, complacency, laziness, sheep mentality

Socialites size people up, building and protecting relationships

Intellectuals assess long term trends to plan working strategies

Energisers push acquiring knowledge to overcome obstacles to success

Integrators push caution and criticism in trusting people and ideas

SEE, ILI, LIE & ESI

All slides are copyrighted by Jack Oliver Aaron of the World Socionics Society

Δ - Delta - The Quadra of Seniority

[Image content]

Accepting & Integrifying Transformers

Questioning elements in Valued Functions

Motivation: to grow and develop themselves, unlocking inner potential

Likes: sincerity, earnestness, practicality, desire to understand others

Dislikes: superficiality, drama, narrowness, neglect

Realists tinker with processes to increase quality of self-sufficient work

Humanitarians explore people’s perspectives to see the best in others

Energisers push a curiosity to keep learning and improving

Integrators push down-to-earth sincerity and harmonising of relationships

LSE, EII, IEE & SLI
